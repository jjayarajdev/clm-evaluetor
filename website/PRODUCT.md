# PRODUCT.md

The brief. Every design and copy decision on the Evaluetor commercial pages

## Product

**Evaluetor** — an AI-native Contract Lifecycle Management platform focused
on the post-signature value zone. We extract obligations, SLAs, risks and
renewal terms automatically; compare contracted terms against operational
reality; and surface the gap before value leaks out.

Built in Rotterdam by Senority B.V. Operating since 2024.

## Audience

European mid-market enterprises buying CLM. Specifically the five
decision-makers and beneficiaries:

- **Legal** — In-house counsel and contract review teams who currently spend
  days reviewing each contract manually.
- **Procurement** — Category managers and vendor-management leaders who want
  to recover service credits and enforce SLAs.
- **Finance** — FP&A and treasury teams who need cash-flow forecasting and
  visibility into auto-renewal exposure.
- **Operations / Vendor Management** — The owners of supplier relationships
  who actually live with the contracts day-to-day.
- **Sales / Account Management** — The owners of *outbound* contracts who
  need to track their own commitments to customers and protect renewal /
  extension revenue. (Steven Visser review, May 2026: page was buy-side
  biased; sell side equally served.)

**Persona depth:** these are not first-time SaaS buyers. They've used
DocuSign CLM, Icertis, or Ironclad and have opinions. They are skeptical of
AI claims. They read carefully and they ask for proof.

### Ideal Customer Profile — first wave (priority for sales motion)

The wider audience is the four-to-five personas above, across mid-to-large
EU enterprises. Within that audience, the highest-ROI early-customer profile
— the first ten customers — should fit this exact shape:

- **Contract base:** €20M – €500M annual contract value under management.
  Below €20M the ROI math is tight; above €500M the deal cycle becomes
  enterprise procurement-led and the pilot motion changes.
- **Buyer:** procurement-led, with CFO and legal involvement in the buying
  committee. Steven's recommendation: lead with procurement (fastest ROI,
  easiest quantification, shortest sales cycle), expand to finance/legal/ops
  after the first credit recovery.
- **Vertical / sector:** high-SLA, high-vendor-spend environments — telco,
  SaaS, managed services, logistics, financial services, pharma. Avoid
  early-stage sectors where contracts are not yet structured.
- **Existing infrastructure:** structured contracts already in a CLM or
  document store; operational data available in ServiceNow, an ERP, or
  ticketing system. Buyers without operational data feeds get less value
  from the connector tier — the leakage detection narrows.
- **Pain signal:** had at least one known leakage event in the past 12
  months — a missed credit, surprise auto-renewal, scope-creep dispute, or
  SLA breach that went uncompensated. Buyers who have not yet experienced
  the pain are educational sales, not transactional.
- **Maturity:** cross-functional collaboration between legal, procurement,
  finance, and operations exists. Buyers who are still siloed will struggle
  to operationalise the platform's outputs.

The first ten customers should fit this profile. Subsequent waves expand
outward to:
- Sell-side / commercial use cases (the Sales & AM persona)
- Smaller portfolios (€5M–€20M)
- Sectors with less structured contract data

## Mood

- Serious, not playful.
- Restrained, not maximalist.
- Evidence-based, not aspirational.
- European-professional, not Silicon-Valley-startup.
- Dutch-modernist (Wim Crouwel, Studio Dumbar lineage) — geometric grid,
  generous whitespace used with purpose, sparing accent color, typography as
  primary visual language.

## Motion depth

Low. Subtle scroll reveal on first appearance, hover state changes on
interactive elements, one rotating headline word. No carousels, no
parallax, no auto-playing video, no scroll-jacking, no confetti.

## Layout family

Editorial-magazine grid. 12-column on desktop. Chapter labels in the left
rail, content in the right (Dutch poster grammar). Hairline horizontal
rules separate sections. Wide negative space between blocks. No glass
morphism, no rounded-corner-everything, no card stacking.

## Register

**Product mode** — the page exists to communicate what the product does and
why it matters. Design choices serve clarity, evidence, and trust.

We are explicitly **not** in **brand mode** (design as the product itself,
visual showmanship as the message). The Evaluetor brand is doing work
underneath; the product page does the talking.

## Anti-references

What we are **not**, and what to refuse if proposed:

- ❌ Silicon Valley startup energy. No "Welcome to Our Platform" energy.
  No exclamation points. No "Let's get started!" copy. No friendly mascots.
- ❌ Legal-tech maximalism. No leather-bound book imagery. No serif-
  everywhere "law firm" cosplay. No gold accents.
- ❌ AI hype. No "powered by AI" badges as decoration. No "next-generation"
  or "revolutionary." No copy that focuses on the AI more than the outcome.
- ❌ Coral red, purple gradients, or other accent colors. Orange only.
- ❌ Inter as primary typeface. Geist as primary; Geist Mono for technical
  data; Instrument Serif only as italic emphasis spans inside larger sans
  headlines. Never Instrument Serif as a primary h1.
- ❌ Eyebrow chips (uppercase mono caps directly above the h1). Chapter
  labels live in the **section grid**, not floating above the headline.
- ❌ Side-stripe cards (a thick orange border-top on a white card). This is
  the Notion/Linear/Stripe-era trope.
- ❌ "Learn more →" as a generic CTA. Every CTA names what it does.
- ❌ Lorem ipsum, `[TBD]`, "Coming soon," dashed-border placeholders.
  Output ships complete or it does not ship.
- ❌ Customer logo carousels of brands we have no relationship with. No
  fake social proof.
- ❌ Stat claims without a source we can defend. Industry numbers must be
  hedged or footnoted; outcome numbers labeled as "targets" until we have
  customer data.

## Sacred (do not change without an explicit decision)

- The accent color is **Dutch orange `#E94E1B`** for marks/dots/underlines,
  **`#C73E0F`** for filled CTA backgrounds (WCAG AA on white). One accent.
- The primary typeface is **Geist**. The technical/data face is **Geist
  Mono**. The italic emphasis face is **Instrument Serif**.
- The page background is warm paper `#F7F6F3`. Surfaces above are
  `#FFFFFF`. Ink is `#0E0E10`.
- The narrative arc is fixed: **the problem (value leakage post-signature)
  → the platform → the four disciplines → the comparison → the begin**.
  Sections may be added or merged but the arc does not reverse.

## Decision-making register

When in doubt, prefer:
- The simpler choice over the cleverer one.
- The honest claim over the bigger claim.
- Typography over iconography.
- Whitespace over a card.
- One accent moment over three.
- A measurable number over an inspirational adjective.
